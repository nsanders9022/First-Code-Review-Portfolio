Employment

Eventually I became an administrative assistant at an architecture firm in Portland

Why Programming?

My master's degree focused on Human Factors, often times refered to as UX, which involves applying psychology to design to make the product or software more intuitive for the user. I really liked the idea of this field and I wanted to apply the knowledge I had gained at a technology company to help increase the usability of their software products

After about two years of working at any job I could obtain while trying to get my foot into this field, I finally decided that I needed to focus on learning more hard skills like programming.

I had taken a Python class during my undergraduate career and a HTML/CSS class in graduate school and greatly enjoyed both courses. While I had plenty of time during my administrative position to teach myself to code using free online resources, I knew that I needed a bootcamp to get me the skills I need to finally get a career as a web developer.

Growing Up

I have been extremely fortunate to have lived abroad throughout my childhood

Shortly after I was born in New Jersey I moved to Basel, Switzerland for the first time. After living there for a couple years my family and I hopped over the water to settle down in a town an hour north of London England. I began school here and kept my English accent for many years after the four years that we spent in England.

Next, my family hopped over more water and we landed in New Jersey for another 4 years. Afterwards, we jumped back over to Basel, Switzerland where I spent the most incredible three years getting to make great friends with people from numerous countries and continents

After three years back in Switzerland my parents had enough of the expat life and settled down in Bellevue, WA, where I attended high school.

My childhood instilled in me a love to travel and continued with that passion by studing abroad in Florence, Italy for a year as well as taking a couple of internation vacations with my boyfriend over the past few years. I am looking forward to being able to see even more of the world in the future
