Why Programming?

My master's degree focused on Human Factors, often times refered to as UX, which involves applying psychology to design to make the product or software more intuitive for the user. I really liked the idea of this field and I wanted to apply the knowledge I had gained at a technology company to help increase the usability of their software products

After about two years of working at any job I could obtain while trying to get my foot into this field, I finally decided that I needed to focus on learning more hard skills like programming.

I had taken a Python class during my undergraduate career and a HTML/CSS class in graduate school and greatly enjoyed both courses. While I had plenty of time during my administrative position to teach myself to code using free online resources, I knew that I needed a bootcamp to get me the skills I need to finally get a career as a web developer. Thanks Epicodus!
